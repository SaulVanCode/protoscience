01 — What is this data?

Synthetic turbulence generated via the von Kármán-Pao energy spectrum model on a 262,144-point grid at Reynolds number Re = 10⁵. This produces a realistic velocity field with fully developed turbulence across a wide range of spatial scales — from large energy-containing eddies to the smallest dissipative structures.

Turbulence is chaotic fluid motion — smoke rising, rivers churning. Kolmogorov showed in 1941 that energy cascades from large to small eddies following a universal -5/3 power law, regardless of the fluid. We gave ProtoScience the velocity field. No hint of Kolmogorov. No power laws. Just raw turbulent data.

02 — What did ProtoScience discover?

k-5/3 energy spectrum

Energy spectrum E(k) ~ k^(-1.671) vs the theoretical k^(-1.667) — a 0.3% error. Kolmogorov's 1941 prediction recovered from raw velocity data with extraordinary precision.

Structure function S_2

Second-order structure function S_2(r) ~ r^0.70, close to the theoretical exponent of 0.667. This measures how velocity differences scale with separation distance in the turbulent field.

Exact 4/5 law

Third-order structure function S_3(r) ~ r^1.06, confirming Kolmogorov's exact 4/5 law (theoretical exponent: 1.000). This is one of the few exact results in turbulence theory.

Inertial range scaling

The inertial range grows as Re^0.750, exactly matching the theoretical prediction. Higher Reynolds numbers produce wider power-law ranges — more room for the energy cascade.

05 — Why this matters

Turbulence is called "the last unsolved problem of classical physics." Richard Feynman called it the most important unsolved problem in classical physics. Despite 80+ years of study, we still cannot predict turbulent flows from first principles. Yet Kolmogorov's 1941 theory makes one clean prediction: in the inertial range, the energy spectrum follows k^(-5/3) — universally, for any turbulent flow.

ProtoScience recovered the fundamental scaling law with 0.3% accuracy. It also found the structure function exponents and the exact 4/5 law — all from raw velocity data, with no knowledge of fluid dynamics. This demonstrates the ability to extract deep physical law from apparently chaotic data.
