01 — What is this data?

The Pantheon+ SH0ES dataset contains 1,590 Type Ia supernovae spanning redshifts from z = 0.01 to z = 2.26 — covering billions of years of cosmic expansion history.

Type Ia supernovae are stellar explosions that all reach roughly the same peak brightness — "standard candles." By comparing how bright they appear vs how bright they should be, we measure their distance. Combined with their redshift (how fast they're moving away), we can map the expansion history of the universe.

02 — What did ProtoScience discover?

+0.52 mag dimmer at high z

High-redshift supernovae (z > 0.3) are +0.52 magnitudes dimmer than a linear Hubble law predicts — meaning they are farther away than expected in a constantly expanding universe.

Λ-CDM best fit

Ωm = 0.349, ΩΛ = 0.651, H0 = 73.0 km/s/Mpc. Literature values: 0.334, 73.6 — recovered within 5% of published results.

q0 = −0.476

The deceleration parameter is negative — the universe is accelerating. q0 = ½Ωm − ΩΛ = −0.476, confirming that dark energy dominates the cosmic energy budget.

Δχ² = 38 over empty universe

Λ-CDM is preferred over an empty (coasting) universe by Δχ² = 38. The data overwhelmingly favor a universe dominated by dark energy.
