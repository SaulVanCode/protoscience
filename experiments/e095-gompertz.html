01 — What is this data?

Actuarial life tables record the probability of dying at each age. These tables are the foundation of life insurance, pension systems, and public health planning. We used data from the US Social Security Administration and WHO estimates for Japan, Nigeria, and Sweden.

In 1825, Benjamin Gompertz discovered that after early childhood, the human death rate increases exponentially with age. ProtoScience received raw mortality rates by age — no mention of Gompertz, no exponential hints. Just numbers.

02 — What did ProtoScience discover?

Gompertz Law

m(x) = α · eβx. After age 30, your mortality rate grows exponentially. R² > 0.993 in every country tested. This is one of the most robust laws in all of biology.

Universal doubling time

Mortality doubles every 7.8 years on average. This holds from the US to Japan to Nigeria — despite vastly different healthcare systems, diets, and genetics.

Development gap

Nigeria's doubling time is 10.3 years vs Japan's 6.6. High baseline mortality "compresses" the exponential. The rich die slower, but the math is the same.

Sex difference

US females: 7.3 years. US males: 8.1 years. Women's mortality accelerates faster but starts from a lower base — net result: women live longer.

04 — Why it matters

The Gompertz law is 200 years old and still no one fully understands why mortality is exponential. Theories range from accumulated DNA damage to telomere shortening to entropy increase in biological systems. The mathematical regularity across all human populations suggests a deep, universal aging process.
