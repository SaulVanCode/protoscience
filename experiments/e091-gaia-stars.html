04 — Why it matters

The Stefan-Boltzmann law is the foundation of stellar astrophysics — it connects a star's luminosity to its size and temperature through fundamental thermodynamics. ProtoScience rediscovered it from Gaia data with exponents accurate to three decimal places, without any prior knowledge of blackbody radiation.

The mass-luminosity relation, which took decades of observational work to establish, emerged naturally with the correct mass-dependent slopes. This demonstrates that ProtoScience can recover not just single laws but the full structure of a physical domain — including the regime-dependent behavior that makes astrophysics rich and complex.
