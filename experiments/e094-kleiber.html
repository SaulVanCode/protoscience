01 — What is this data?

PanTHERIA is the most comprehensive database of mammalian life history traits ever compiled. It contains data on 5,416 species — from the 2-gram bumblebee bat to the 150-tonne blue whale — including body mass, metabolic rate, longevity, gestation period, litter size, home range, and population density.

In 1932, Max Kleiber discovered that an animal's metabolic rate scales with its body mass to the 3/4 power. This "quarter-power scaling" has been called the most universal law in biology. We gave ProtoScience the raw PanTHERIA data with no hints about Kleiber or any biological theory.

02 — What did ProtoScience discover?

Kleiber's Law

BMR = 3.90 · M0.702 (expected exponent: 0.75). From 573 species with measured metabolic rates. R²=0.923. A mouse and an elephant obey the same equation.

Population density

Pop density ~ M−0.741 (expected: −0.75). From 947 species. Bigger animals need exponentially more space. Error from theory: 0.9%.

Home range scaling

Home range ~ M1.061 (expected: 1.0). From 700 species. Territory size scales linearly with body mass — a fundamental ecological constraint.

Quarter-power universality

All discovered exponents cluster near multiples of 1/4: 0.70 ≈ 3/4, 0.20 ≈ 1/4, 0.19 ≈ 1/4, −0.74 ≈ −3/4. Biology runs on quarter powers.

04 — Why it matters

Kleiber's Law is one of the most debated equations in biology. The 3/4 exponent was first measured empirically in 1932, and West, Brown & Enquist proposed a theoretical explanation in 1997 based on fractal branching networks (circulatory systems). The debate over whether the "true" exponent is 3/4 or 2/3 continues today.

ProtoScience found an exponent of 0.702 from 573 species — sitting between the two competing theories. More importantly, it discovered that the same quarter-power pattern appears in longevity, gestation, home range, and population density, suggesting a deep mathematical unity underlying all of mammalian biology.

This is the first time ProtoScience has been applied to biology. The fact that the same power-law fitting pipeline that rediscovered Kepler's Laws and Einstein's equations also works on mammals demonstrates the universality of the approach.
