01 — What is this data?

JPL's Small-Body Database contains orbital parameters for every known asteroid. We pulled 10,000 main-belt asteroids with their semi-major axes (distance from the Sun), orbital periods, and absolute magnitudes (a proxy for size). The asteroid belt sits between Mars (~1.5 AU) and Jupiter (~5.2 AU), but it is not a uniform disk — it has structure carved by Jupiter's gravity.

We asked ProtoScience to analyze the orbital distribution. No mention of Jupiter. No resonance theory. Just 10,000 orbits.

05 — Why this matters

The Kirkwood gaps are one of the most beautiful examples of gravitational dynamics in nature. An asteroid at exactly 2.50 AU orbits the Sun three times for every one Jupiter orbit. Each time Jupiter passes, it gives the asteroid a small gravitational kick at the same point in its orbit — and over millions of years, these kicks accumulate until the asteroid is flung out of the gap entirely.

ProtoScience detected all five gaps and correctly associated them with integer period ratios — without knowing Jupiter exists. This demonstrates the ability to find causal structure (an unseen perturber) from statistical signatures in orbital data.
