Rediscovering Kepler's Laws from Exoplanet Data

Given only orbital periods, semi-major axes, and stellar masses for 2,833 confirmed exoplanets, ProtoScience blindly recovered Kepler's Third Law with R² = 0.998 — a relationship first published in 1619, now rediscovered autonomously from NASA data.

Kepler's Third Law: P² = a³ / M★

Mass-Radius Relation

Kepler III  P² = 0.992 · a³/M★    slope = 0.9988 R² = 0.9982
P vs a     P ~ a1.457 (Kepler predicts 1.500) R² = 0.9891
T_eq law   Teq = 0.992 · T★ · √(R★/2a) R² = 0.9676
M-R       R ~ 1.29 · M0.370 (all), M0.416 (rocky), M0.052 (gas giants) R² = 0.744

Kepler recovered exactly

Slope = 0.9988 (expect 1.0), k = 0.992 (expect 1.0). Error of just 0.12% across 2,833 planetary systems spanning 8 orders of magnitude in orbital period.

Two planet populations

Rocky planets: R ~ M0.42. Gas giants: R ~ M0.05. The system autonomously discovered that giant planets barely grow in radius as mass increases — a signature of electron degeneracy pressure.

Temperature from first principles

Teq predicted with R² = 0.968 using only stellar temperature, stellar radius, and orbital distance. Scale factor 0.992 implies near-zero average albedo.
